Where are Indigenous populations in Canada?

Information

The data visualization is a map demonstrating the percentage of indigenous groups in each territory/province. The indigenous population in Canada is concentrated in the North. As demonstrated in the visualization, the Nunavut Province and Northwest Territories have a predominantly indigenous population. Both regions have all three indigenous groups in its population. In the Nunavut province, there is a greater number of Inuit population. In the Northwest territory, it mainly consists of First Nations people.
